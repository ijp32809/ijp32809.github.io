Ain't Nothing Finer in the Land

Football at the University of Georgia is a staple of the city of Athens and arguably the pinacle of the University of Georgia itself. Bulldog football has been existence since 1892 when Dr. Charles Herty a chemistry professor at the time for the university gathered a team of students and hosted the university innagural game against Mercer University on January 30, 1892. Since this time the University of Georgia has seen quite a bit of success in the realm of collegiate football. Over the past century the Dawgs have won two national championships, 12 SEC championships, had two Heisman trophy winners, and are placed 11th overall in the NCAA in all time wins.

Heisman Winners

Herschel Walker (left) played for the University of Georgia from 1980-1983 and is widely considered one of the greatest college running backs of all time. As a collegiate athlete Walker was a part of the 1980 National Championship season when the Dawgs defeated the Notre Dame Fighting Irish in the Sugar Bowl, and is the only the history of NCAA to finish in the top three in Heisman voting in all 3 of his collegiate seasons. Walker holds a substanial amount of records for the University of Georgia including all time rushing yards, all time rushing touchdowns, and average yards per game.
